Chapter: An Empty Manor
Character: Thomas Moody
Dawn comes when I wake up, stiff in my watch chair.
I can't believe I fell asleep.
Captain Sinha is looking at me with a hint of reproach.
Sushil Sinha: I see you managed a little sleep, Mr Moody.
Sushil Sinha: It is alright, I did not.
Sushil Sinha: Do not worry, nothing happened during the night.
Thomas Moody: Good. I am sorry, I did not think I would fall asleep.
Sushil Sinha: It happens. Do not blame yourself too much over it.
I look around the room. Everyone is awake now, all of them dishevelled.
Doctor Baldwin is in particularly bad shape, having contracted a sort of fever.
Samuel Manning is already back to drinking from his pocket flask.
The ladies have managed to make themselves presentable, and Captain Sinha looks as though he had a normal night's sleep.
I wonder what I look like, but it hardly matters now.
It is time for action, and Captain Sinha takes the lead.
Sushil Sinha: Alright, now that everyone is awake, I think it is time we planned our next move.
People murmur in agreement.
Sushil Sinha: Good. What I suggest is this.
Sushil Sinha: Let us go quickly to our rooms and get ready for the long walk.
Sushil Sinha: We can meet afterwards in the entrance hall.
Sushil Sinha: The sooner we leave, the better.
Amelia Baxter: Captain, Miss Marsh and I have just had a discussion.
Amelia Baxter: And we believe we shall not make it.
Sushil Sinha: Not make it, Miss Baxter?
Sushil Sinha: It is a long journey I'll admit it, but not a difficult one, merely following the road.
Sushil Sinha: And we will walk slowly, and make stops as you need.
Amelia Baxter: I know it looks like an easy stroll for you captain.
Amelia Baxter: My condition is not what it used to be nor am I equipped for that type of activity.
Rosalind Marsh: And I am no better.
Rosalind Marsh: My health has never been strong, and a whole day on a wet road would be very taxing.
Captain Sinha is considering this, then turns towards me.
Sushil Sinha: Maybe they are right Mr Moody.
Sushil Sinha: This journey might not be fit for the ladies, it would be best if they stay there.
Sushil Sinha: And even the man might not be in perfect shape.
He looks at Samuel Manning, and Doctor Baldwin.
They both look sickly and exhausted.
Sushil Sinha: We would go definitely faster just the two of us.
Sushil Sinha: We could fetch help and be back before sundown.
Sushil Sinha: What do you think?
Something in me refuses it, flatly, I could not tell why.
If we do not leave together, something terrible will happen.
But how can I convince everyone of this?

Whatever I said was not convincing enough.
Amelia Baxter: I am sorry, Mr Moody. Truly.
Amelia Baxter: But none of that is a reason to march me into a wood.
Rosalind Marsh: Nor me.
Sushil Sinha: Mr Moody, we cannot stand here arguing until sundown.
And there it is.
I have spent everything I had and moved not one of them an inch.
The dread has not left me. It has only stopped being any use.
Thomas Moody: Very well, Captain.
Thomas Moody: The two of us, then.

Rosalind Marsh: We will stay here for as long as needed, do not worry.

Sushil Sinha: Mr Moody.
Thomas Moody: Yes, let us go.
