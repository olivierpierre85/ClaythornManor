Chapter: An Empty Manor
Character: Daniel Baldwin
I wake up to the soft sound of someone entering the room.
Miss Marsh is coming in.
Rosalind Marsh: I've just come from the dinner room, it's empty.
Rosalind Marsh: In fact I didn't see anyone anywhere.
Rosalind Marsh: No staff at all.
Daniel Baldwin: How is it possible?
Daniel Baldwin: Where could everyone have gone?
Daniel Baldwin: That doesn't make sense.
Rosalind Marsh: I don't know, but it is indeed very suspicious.
Rosalind Marsh: We were right to be cautious, something weird is definitely happening here.
Daniel Baldwin: But what should we do then?
Daniel Baldwin: Should we...
Rosalind Marsh: Shh. I hear someone coming.
She puts her ear to the door.
I get up quietly and join her.
We hear two people walking together, talking in low voices.
I recognise Ted Harring and Amelia Baxter.
After they have passed, Rosalind Marsh confirms it.
Rosalind Marsh: Ted Harring and Amelia Baxter.
Rosalind Marsh: What are they doing here? There is no reason for them to pass by my door.
Rosalind Marsh: I don't like this.
Daniel Baldwin: Maybe they're just looking for the staff.
Daniel Baldwin: They must be as surprised as we are.
Rosalind Marsh: Maybe, but I don't know.
Rosalind Marsh: It could be something else entirely.
Rosalind Marsh: I don't really trust those two.
Rosalind Marsh: I prefer if we avoided them at the moment.
Daniel Baldwin: If it helps keep your mind at ease, why not.
Daniel Baldwin: But what should we do instead?
Rosalind Marsh: Well, we can't stay in this room.
Rosalind Marsh: They might come looking here eventually.
Daniel Baldwin: So what do you propose?
Rosalind Marsh: I know a place where we can hide for a while until we know more.
Daniel Baldwin: Very well. Where are we heading?
Rosalind Marsh: Come with me, I'll show you.
Without waiting for an answer she slightly opens the door and takes a peek outside.
Rosalind Marsh: The hallway is clear. Come with me.
Daniel Baldwin: Fine.
We make it only a few steps before we hear another set of footsteps.
Miss Marsh pulls me into a narrow recess beside a service door.
I press my back against the cold wood, holding my breath.
Captain Sinha appears in the corridor, moving with quiet purpose.
He pauses for a moment, as if listening, then continues on his way downstairs.
I glance at Miss Marsh.
She is perfectly still, her breathing shallow and controlled.
She knows exactly how to make herself invisible.
It is impressive, and slightly unsettling.
It feels like this is not the first time she has had to hide like this.
When the danger is over, she signals for me to follow her.
We take a flight of stairs up to the attic.
Rosalind Marsh: Perfect. There is a room here where we will be comfortable.
Rosalind Marsh: I don't think they would look here.
She unlocks the door next to us and goes in.
Rosalind Marsh: Come on in, Doctor, it's empty.
I follow her, and she immediately closes the door behind me.
Rosalind Marsh: We should sit tight here for a bit.
Rosalind Marsh: My guess is that they will try to look everywhere but here.
Rosalind Marsh: In a while, either they'll have found someone or realise we are the only ones left in the Manor.
Rosalind Marsh: We can go check again then.
She thought of everything. I don't think I would have acted that way on my own.
It's unsettling.
Daniel Baldwin: Excuse me, but how did you get the key to this room?
Rosalind Marsh: Oh, I found it in the main hall earlier this morning, on a table.
Rosalind Marsh: It's a pass that opens all the doors in the mansion apparently.
Rosalind Marsh: Wherever he went, the butler didn't think he would need his key anymore.
Daniel Baldwin: Right, so he's probably gone for good then.
Rosalind Marsh: That's what I think, and the rest of the staff probably went with him.
Daniel Baldwin: As well as Lady Claythorn.
Rosalind Marsh: Yes, it's very likely.
