Chapter: An Empty Manor
Character: Daniel Baldwin
Sushil Sinha: Doctor Baldwin.
Sushil Sinha: Doctor Baldwin, are you awake?
I slowly open my eyes to find Captain Sinha standing over me.
Daniel Baldwin: Yes, Captain.
Daniel Baldwin: I am awake.
Sushil Sinha: I apologise for the intrusion, Doctor, but it is already well past eight o'clock.
Sushil Sinha: I believe we should leave the room without delay.
Daniel Baldwin: Very well.
Daniel Baldwin: Give me a moment.
Sushil Sinha: Of course.
A quick glance at Sushil Sinha tells me he is perfectly prepared.
I do not know when he woke up, but he must have been waiting for quite some time.
In contrast, I must look a terrible sight.
I have been sweating all night, my body aching for a dose of laudanum.
Fortunately, I was not in my own room, or I might have relapsed.
I can only hope that today will be easier.
Sushil Sinha: I suggest we go to the dining room first.
Sushil Sinha: We should see who is already awake.
Daniel Baldwin: That seems reasonable.
I make myself as presentable as I can, and we set off together.
Sushil Sinha: This is odd.
Sushil Sinha: There is no one here.
Daniel Baldwin: And breakfast has not been laid out either.
Sushil Sinha: Which is unusual, considering the hour.
Daniel Baldwin: Indeed.
Daniel Baldwin: Perhaps we should look elsewhere.
Sushil Sinha: I agree.
Sushil Sinha: The kitchen would be the logical next stop.
Daniel Baldwin: Let us go.
Sushil Sinha: It is completely silent.
Sushil Sinha: That is not normal.
Sushil Sinha: Something is very wrong.
Sushil Sinha: We should proceed with caution and search the house for the others.
Daniel Baldwin: I was thinking the same.
We search the manor methodically for some time.
Room after room yields nothing, and we begin to lose hope.
Then, as we descend the stairs to the entrance hall, we hear the faint sound of voices below.
Sushil Sinha: Is anyone here?
Ted Harring: Captain Sinha, is that you?
We look down and spot Ted Harring and Amelia Baxter.
Sushil Sinha: At last, living, breathing souls.
Sushil Sinha: We were beginning to feel as though we were wandering a ghost house.
Amelia Baxter: We feel the same.
Amelia Baxter: You are the first people we have encountered all morning.
Amelia Baxter: I cannot understand where everyone else could have gone.
Sushil Sinha: Nor can we.
Daniel Baldwin: There is one place we have not checked.
Daniel Baldwin: Miss Marsh's room.
Amelia Baxter: I knocked earlier.
Amelia Baxter: There was no answer.
Amelia Baxter: I tried to enter, but it was locked.
Sushil Sinha: Yes, we received no answer as well.
Sushil Sinha: I think it is time we check anyway.
We all stand in front of Miss Marsh's room.
Captain Sinha tests the handle.
Sushil Sinha: Locked.
Sushil Sinha: Miss Marsh?
Sushil Sinha: Are you inside?
There is no reply.
Sushil Sinha: Doctor, Mister Harring,
Sushil Sinha: I believe if the three of us shove together, the door will give way.
Amelia Baxter: Are you sure it is wise to do this?
Sushil Sinha: I am afraid that under the circumstances, we have no other choice.
Amelia Baxter: Very well.
Sushil Sinha: Lads, on my command.
Sushil Sinha: One.
Sushil Sinha: Two.
Sushil Sinha: Three.
The lock gives way and the door swings open.
We all enter the room.
Sushil Sinha: There is nobody here.
Ted Harring: So she just vanished?
Ted Harring: Along with half the house?
Sushil Sinha: It would appear so.
A heavy silence settles over us.
Sushil Sinha: I do not understand what is happening.
Sushil Sinha: We need to discuss this.
Amelia Baxter: Of course.
Amelia Baxter: Perhaps we should settle somewhere more comfortable, like the tea room?
Sushil Sinha: A good idea.
