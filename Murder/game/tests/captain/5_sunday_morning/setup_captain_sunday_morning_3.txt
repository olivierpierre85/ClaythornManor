Chapter: An Empty Manor
Character: Sushil Sinha
I sleep poorly, and only in patches.
The house stays quiet through the night.
A little before half past eight, I dress and sit on the edge of the bed.
There is a soft, quick knock at the door.
Rosalind Marsh: Captain. It's me.
I unlock the door.
Miss Marsh slips inside without a word and closes it behind her.
She is pale, but quite collected.
Sushil Sinha: Miss Marsh.
Sushil Sinha: Are you all right?
Rosalind Marsh: I am, but there is something wrong with the house.
Sushil Sinha: What do you mean?
Rosalind Marsh: There are no staff. No fires lit. No breakfast laid.
Rosalind Marsh: I have walked the dining room, the kitchen, the back corridor.
Rosalind Marsh: Not a soul.
Sushil Sinha: So I was right, something strange is happening here.
Sushil Sinha: Have you checked any of the other rooms?
Rosalind Marsh: No, I came straight to you.
Sushil Sinha: A sound choice.
Sushil Sinha: It might not be safe for you to just roam around the house.
Rosalind Marsh: I believe you are right.
Rosalind Marsh: I was thinking we might hide for a while, at least until the police come.
Sushil Sinha: Right, the police.
Sushil Sinha: I do not know for sure they will come, Miss Marsh.
Sushil Sinha: There is a good chance they were never called in the first place.
Rosalind Marsh: Never called, but Lady Claythorn...
Rosalind Marsh: Oh...
Sushil Sinha: Yes, if Lady Claythorn is behind this masquerade, and there is a very good chance that she is, there was no reason for her to call the police.
Sushil Sinha: We might wait here indefinitely.
Rosalind Marsh: What can we do then?
Sushil Sinha: I see two options:
Sushil Sinha: First, we could search this place and try to make sense of what is happening.
Sushil Sinha: I have a gun, so I can protect myself.
Rosalind Marsh: I do not like the idea of roaming around the house, even with you to protect me.
Rosalind Marsh: What is the second option?
Sushil Sinha: We could leave right away, on foot if we must.
Rosalind Marsh: On foot, Captain?
Sushil Sinha: I am afraid yes.
Sushil Sinha: Lady Claythorn and her staff most likely left with the motor car.
Sushil Sinha: It is a fairly long walk to the town, but it is still early, I am sure we can make it.
Rosalind Marsh: Oh, I am sure you could, Captain.
Rosalind Marsh: Sadly I can't.
She says it quietly, and I see now what I had been too preoccupied to mark before.
The pallor that is not only fear.
The careful way she holds herself.
Rosalind Marsh: I am not a healthy woman, Captain.
Rosalind Marsh: There is a good chance my body will not hold for such a long journey.
Sushil Sinha: I see.
I think her condition might be worse than her quiet behaviour shows.
But if she does not want to tell me more, I will not press her.
Sushil Sinha: In that case, I believe the safer bet is for me to leave alone.
Sushil Sinha: And I should go as soon as possible then, this way there is a very good chance that I can come back for you before the end of the day.
Sushil Sinha: You could hide until then.
Rosalind Marsh: I see.
Rosalind Marsh: If I am being honest, I do not like this, Captain.
Rosalind Marsh: It feels extremely dangerous for me.
Sushil Sinha: I know and I am sorry, but I still believe it is our best chance.
Sushil Sinha: And if it can ease your mind, you can take this pistol.
Sushil Sinha: I try to always keep it on me, one never knows when one might need protection.
Sushil Sinha: Do you know how to use one?
Rosalind Marsh: I can manage, I believe.
Rosalind Marsh: But I really hope I won't have to use it.
Sushil Sinha: You probably won't, just find a safe hidden place, close the door and do not open to anybody.
Sushil Sinha: It is safe to say that everyone is a suspect at this point.
Rosalind Marsh: You are right.
Rosalind Marsh: I believe the attic is the best place to hide.
Rosalind Marsh: It should be empty now.
Sushil Sinha: The attic, good idea.
Sushil Sinha: I have the butler's master key.
Sushil Sinha: Use it to open a room and hide in it.
I hand her the key, I do not need it anymore anyway.
Rosalind Marsh: All right, I think I can manage that.
Rosalind Marsh: Thank you, Captain.
Sushil Sinha: Great, in that case I see no reason to linger.
Sushil Sinha: I will go straight away and come back as soon as possible.
Sushil Sinha: Good luck, Miss Marsh.
Rosalind Marsh: Good luck, Captain.
I do not waste time and go down fast to the entrance hall.
It is still empty, so I make it outside.
I take only my coat and what will fit in its pockets.
The drive gives way to a rough road, and the road to open country between the trees.
I keep a solid pace for an hour, then, away to my right, I notice something.
A shape that is the wrong colour for bark.
And from that shape, I can spot the barrel of a rifle.
I quickly grab for my own gun but come up empty.
Miss Marsh has it.
I am defenceless.
You set out alone, on foot, into open country you did not know.
A soldier ought to have known better than to cross such ground without cover.
But you were no soldier, whatever the medals said.
The shot came from the treeline, and you did not hear the second one.
