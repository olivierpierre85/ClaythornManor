Chapter: The Hunt
Character: Rosalind Marsh
I am quite content to remain behind.
It will allow me to rest.
Even though I should at least join Mrs Baxter for lunch.
And I could take the opportunity that most people are outside to "look" for treasure.
The priority would be to find the stash of money promised to everyone.
But, if as I suspect, there isn't any, I am sure I can find other valuables in here.
Once I feel I have found enough, I might be able to leave early and avoid any suspicion.
Map Choice: Kitchen
I move quietly through the back passage.
Years of moving through dark passages in hospitals and large houses have taught me how to remain unseen.
I reach the kitchen door and ease it open an inch.
I almost step inside until I notice one of the maids, alone.
It is better if she does not see me.
I pull the door to and leave.
Map Choice: Garage
I reach the garage, making sure I should not encounter anyone.
It is a cold, oil-smelling place.
There is an old car; it does not look as though it is working any more.
Tools hang neatly along one wall, and a bicycle leans against another.
Nothing here that is of any use to me.
Map Choice: Gun room
The gun room.
I am certain that I should not be here, so I made sure no one noticed me.
Shotguns and hunting rifles line three walls, arranged on open racks.
The smell of gun oil is sharp.
Everything is laid out as though ready for use — nothing locked away.
There are even a few small handguns lying on a table.
It would be foolish not to take one.
Menu Choice: Take a pistol
My hand closes around the grip of a small revolver.
It is not loaded.
And I have not seen any bullets lying about.
Nevertheless, it could prove useful.
I slip it into my bag.
Nobody will notice. Not for a while, at any rate.
Map Choice: Wait for luncheon in the Tea Room
I do not really have the strength to explore.
I should just wait for luncheon here.
Amelia Baxter comes a bit later.
She is composed as ever when she arrives.
She takes the chair across from me.
Amelia Baxter: Miss Marsh, how are you?
Rosalind Marsh: Fine, thank you.
Amelia Baxter: It seems we have not yet had a proper chance to talk, have we?
Rosalind Marsh: Indeed.
Menu Choice: What do you think of the other guests?
Amelia Baxter: I haven't had time to form an opinion of everyone yet.
Amelia Baxter: I have only had time to speak with Captain Sinha, and with that poor drunkard Samuel Manning.
Amelia Baxter: And I could also exchange a few words with our hostess, Lady Claythorn.
Menu Choice: What do you think of Lady Claythorn?
Amelia Baxter: I only exchanged a few pleasantries with the lady of the house.
Amelia Baxter: She seems delightful to me.
Amelia Baxter: What pleased me even more was that she addressed me as an equal.
Amelia Baxter: That's very different from most noble people I've met.
Menu Choice: Talk about something else
Menu Choice: You don't have anymore questions for her
A member of the household staff steps in quietly.
Young woman: Excuse me for interrupting, but do you ladies need some lunch?
Rosalind Marsh: That would be lovely, thank you.
She returns shortly with some plates.
We eat and exchange pleasantries.
When we have finished, I set my napkin aside.
I have been feeling out of sorts all morning, and the feeling has not quite left me.
A cough escapes me, sharper than I expected.
Rosalind Marsh: I am feeling rather poorly, I am afraid.
Rosalind Marsh: I think I shall retire to my room for a short while.
Rosalind Marsh: Would you mind being left on your own?
Amelia Baxter: Not at all, please don't concern yourself.
Amelia Baxter: There is more than enough here to occupy me.
I leave her to it and make my way back upstairs.
A brief rest should do me good before the hunting party returns.
I rested a while, I need to get some strength if I want to get through the day.
After a little while, I am starting to feel better.
Perhaps I can use the remaining time productively.
Map Choice: Dining Room
The dining room is empty.
The table is not entirely cleared.
Strange, that should have been done by now.
Looks like the manor is understaffed.
Map Choice: Billiard room
The billiard room is empty, the table undisturbed.
There is nothing here for me.
Map Choice: Entrance Hall
The great hall is quiet.
The chandelier has been turned down low.
I stand beneath it for a moment, listening.
The house settles around me.
There is something watchful about the place at this hour.
Map Choice: Garden
The sky is a little brighter this morning. Not much, but enough.
The paths are damp underfoot, and there is a chill still in the air, but it is pleasant to be outside.
I walk the length of the garden slowly, not pushing myself.
The house is quiet behind me. It is a relief, for a moment, to be away from it.
I do not stay long. One circuit, and then back inside.
A commotion from the entrance hall draws my attention.
It seems the hunting party has returned.
