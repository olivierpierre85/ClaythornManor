# 🎭 Story & Characters

## Backstory

1901 - Boxer Rebellion (Officer and Captain)

In China, a young girl is saved by a **young officer** during the Boxer Rebellion. They grow close, and she falls in love.

A **Nurse** there was also in love with the officer. Out of jealousy, she ratted her out to management.

The **Captain** receives the news. He then reassigns the young officer back to another company, for the sake of the girl's honour (because he is a nobleman, etc.).!!!!!! The captain has no real power back then, he will just transfer the information to a "real" officer!!!!!

The woman is left pregnant. In 1902, she gives birth to the **young Lad**. She wants to keep the baby, but he is stolen by the doctor in charge, who claims she would not be a decent mother. She does not know where he has been taken. The **Doctor** sends the baby to an orphanage in England.

She returns home to try and find him, but is cast out by her family. She has to become a maid for rich families, and is an aspiring actress in her spare time. She changes employers frequently, even working for a time at Claythorn Manor, as she never loses hope of finding her son. She inspects every orphanage in England, finding employment near them. It takes years, and when she finally finds the right orphanage... 

There is the name of a young boy with the right date of birth in their archives, but she is arrested in the process before being able to learn where he lived. The **Drunk** lawyer who was supposed to defend her failed. Thus, she was sent away, delaying the moment she could meet her son.

In 1923, she realises that he is still alive when she sees his name in the paper (she avidly reads all the papers in the area) after he saved a child. Her first instinct is to try and find him, but she is afraid he will want nothing to do with her. She is also starting to show symptoms of mental instability, which, given her story, is not surprising.

So, instead, she spies on the persons who were responsible for her unhappiness and swears to make them pay. She will gather them in an empty place (she knows Claythorn Manor is empty as the previous owners have died).

Not content merely to kill them, she will make them suffer. Then, she will reveal her plan to her son and his old love, whom she has also tracked down.

She needs help for her plan, and she manages to find a company of actors needing money—the same company that once rejected her, effectively killing her dream of being famous. She has never met them, but needs to kill them anyway.

**1924 - Current Year**

However, the first part of the plan is that her love does not show up. Someone else has taken his place; she does not realise it at first because of his mask. But when she sees the truth, she kills him.

### 📝 Summary
- **1901**: Young girl saved by officer during Boxer Rebellion. Nurse is jealous and rats her out.
- **1902**: Girl gives birth to the Lad. Doctor steals the baby for money.
- **1923**: Girl (now the Host/Lady Claythorn?) realises her son is alive.
- **1924 (Current)**: She gathers everyone who wronged her at Claythorn Manor to make them pay. Her plan involves her old love (who is actually dead/replaced by Broken Face).

## 📜 Character Bios

### The Lad (Ted Harring)
Born in 1902 (22 in 1924), he was abandoned at birth and raised in an orphanage. 
At the age of approximately 12 to 14,

However, upon reaching the age of 18, and without a good education, he left and began a life as a thief in Birmingham.

One day, whilst robbing a house, he accidentally started a fire. He realised the house was not empty when he heard a baby screaming. He saved the child but left the wet-nurse, who had passed out next to him, to die. The newspapers reported only his heroic deed. A few months later, he received an invitation, ostensibly to reward him for his bravery.

That is the event that triggered everything.

In the manor, he feels (rightfully) out of place, but he tries to make the best of it.


### The Psychic (Amelia Baxter)

The killer (see main background).


### The Doctor (Daniel Baldwin)
A promising young student, he joined the army to learn on the field.
In the chaos of war, and to suppress his homosexual tendencies, he started to abuse narcotics which were easily accessible to him.
During the Boxer Rebellion, he decided to steal the son of a young woman after she gave birth, because her wealthy parents paid him to do so.
Back in England, he found employment at a charity hospital. He kept the position because of the easy access to drugs it provided, often replacing patients' morphine with placebos.
He did not expect his long service ever to be rewarded, but he accepted the invitation to Claythorn Manor nevertheless.

### The Captain (Sushil Sinha)
Takes charge. Bossy. He also comes from nobility, so he is able to realise that the footmen are not very good. 
*Note: An Indian soldier of noble ancestry. The killer has no idea an Indian can be an aristocrat.*
He will explain that, besides himself, nobody is true gentry (most are pretenders, like the Psychic and the Doctor).

An administrative officer who never saw battle, he supposed he was mistaken for another Indian soldier, but accepted the invitation anyway.

"For once, the inability of white people to tell Indians apart was to my advantage."


### The Nurse (Rosalind Marsh)
**The Pseudo-villain**
A nurse who was also in China. She ratted out the Psychic to the Captain regarding the baby (in the hope of having the young officer for herself, perhaps, or simply because she is a prude). Bitter and angry, she lived a lonely life and never married. After nursing in hospitals, she moved on to caring for the elderly, always trying to secure something in their wills.
She assumed she was invited by Lady Claythorn because of her service in the army.

### The Drunk (Samuel Manning)
Drinks because of his deceased wife. He blames an incompetent doctor.
He forgets everything when he wakes up. This leads to a black-out, then a skip to the next day (with an option to drink more). You must find a way to make him stop drinking so that the option not to drink appears.
He might have killed someone whilst drink-driving one day (the Captain's wife? TODO: develop).
Invited because, despite everything, he managed to become a defence lawyer, albeit a terrible one.

### The Broken Face (Thomas Moody / Imposter)
Badly injured in the war, he wears a mask to cover his face.
He is supposed to be the young officer whom the Psychic loved, invited for his service.
However, it turns out that the officer is dead, and a friend saw the invitation made in his name. That is why he stole the papers and put on a mask in case anyone was supposed to recognise him (which turned out to be unnecessary).
Upon his arrival at the manor, the Psychic quickly realises he is not the right person, so she poisons his drink immediately.
He is an amateur sleuth and is skilled at interrogation; he can sneak into certain places.
MOODY and ARCHIE WERE war buddies, both officers, maybe lovers.

### The Host - Lady Claythorn (OR ????)
An out-of-work actress, she stole the job that the Psychic wanted some years ago. It is not a revenge as strong as the others, but as they say, 'you cannot make an omelette without breaking eggs.'
She is hired for a very specific, and theoretically lucrative, job (she received a decent advance to convince her). She is supposed to hire the rest of the staff to organise the weekend.
